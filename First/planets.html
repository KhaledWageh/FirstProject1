Planets

  • There are a lot of planets in a place called space as we said.

  • But the most important group of them is the planets in our Solar System.

  • The Solar System is the gravitationally bound system of the Sun and the objects that orbit it. The largest of such objects are the eight planets.

  • These eight planets are ordered by how far it is from the sun.

1- Mercury:

  • Mercury is the closest to the sun by 49.305 million km.

  • Mercury is the first planet from the Sun and the smallest planet in the Solar System.

  • Mercury is known for the fastest planet because The closer a planet is to the Sun, the faster it travels.

  • The planet Mercury is mainly made up of iron.

2- Venus:

  • Venus is away from the sun by 108.66 million km.

  • Venus is the second planet from the Sun.

  • It is a rocky planet with the densest atmosphere of all the rocky bodies in the Solar System.

  • It is the only planet with a mass and size that is close to that of its orbital neighbour Earth.

3- Earth:

  • Earth is away from the sun by 152.1 million km.

  • Earth is the third planet from the Sun.

  • Earth is the only place known in the universe where life has originated and found habitability.

  • Earth is the only planet known to sustain liquid surface water, with ocean water extending over 70.8% of the planet, making it an ocean world.

4- Mars:

  • Mars is away from the sun by 249.71 million km.

  • Mars is the fourth planet and the furthest terrestrial planet from the Sun.

  • The reddish color of its surface is due to finely grained iron(III) oxide dust in the soil, giving it the nickname "the Red Planet".

  • Mars may look hot, but don't let its color fool you Mars is actually pretty cold! In orbit, Mars is about 50 million miles farther away from the Sun than Earth.

5- Jupiter

  • Jupiter is away from the sun by 741.78 million km.

  • Jupiter is the fifth planet from the Sun and the largest in the Solar System.

  • It is a gas giant with a mass more than two and a half times that of all the other planets in the Solar System combined

  • A year on Jupiter is the same as 11.8 Earth years.

6- Saturn

  • Saturn is away from the sun by 1.4625 billion km.

  • Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and the second-largest in the Solar System, after Jupiter.

  • It is a gas giant with an average radius of about nine and a half times that of Earth.

  • It has only one-eighth the average density of Earth, but is over 95 times more massive.

7- Uranus

  • Uranus is away from the sun by 2.9372 billion km

  • Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun, and has the third-largest diameter in our solar system.

  • Most of the planet is made out of water, ammonia, and methane in a supercritical phase of matter, which in astronomy is called 'ice' or volatiles.

  • It was the first planet found with the aid of a telescope.

  • Uranus was discovered in 1781 by astronomer William Herschel, although he originally thought it was either a comet or a star.

8- Neptune

  • Neptune is the eighth planet from the Sun and the farthest known planet in the Solar System.

  • It is the fourth-largest planet in the Solar System by diameter, the third-most-massive planet, and the densest giant planet.

  • Neptune is one of two ice giants in the outer solar system (the other is Uranus). Most (80% or more) of the planet's mass is made up of a hot dense fluid of "icy" materials water, methane, and ammonia above a small, rocky core. Of the giant planets, Neptune is the densest.

  • It is 17 times the mass of Earth, and slightly more massive than its near-twin Uranus.
